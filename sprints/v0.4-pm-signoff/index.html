    4. Velocity Note¶

    Sprint 4 was LARGE by design (see v0.4-pm-scope.md §7). Scope was EXECUTED within the 2-week envelope with the 4-phase delivery plan intact. The 2 Phase 2 additions (F1-NAV, F1-INGEST-VIEW) were absorbed without phase slippage. G3 was not deferred-to-live — the pattern of accumulated live-run deferrals from v0.3 was broken. One documentation gap (D6) accumulated; this is the primary velocity lesson for Sprint 5: tech-writer work on USER.md and DEPLOY.md must be started at Sprint 5 kickoff and not left to the final phase.

    Sprint 5 scope estimate: F5 (retrieval + citations) is the most complex item; F9 (review queue), F10 (deep research), F12 (multi-format), F13 (cascade delete) are individually scoped but collectively large. Recommend the orchestrator consider a 3-phase plan for M5 as M4 was for M4. Velocity note: M4 proved that 4 features + 2 additions + 1 backend endpoint + 8 GUX sub-items can be delivered in 2 weeks with disciplined phasing. M5 carries 5 major features — phasing is mandatory.
